            Common Questions

            Is there a free trial?

            Every new account gets a free 30-day premium trial. No credit card required. When it ends, you keep your feeds and training on the free tier.

            Can I import feeds from another reader?

            Yes. Upload your OPML file and NewsBlur imports all your subscriptions and folders in seconds. Works with Google Reader exports, Feedly, Inoreader, and more.

            Is NewsBlur open source?

            Fully open source under the MIT license. Run your own instance with Docker, contribute on GitHub, or just read the code. No lock-in, ever.

            What happens if I cancel?

            Your account reverts to the free tier. You keep all your feeds (up to 64), training, and saved stories. No data is deleted. Upgrade again anytime and everything is restored.

            Does NewsBlur work with other apps?

            Yes. Full sync with Reeder, ReadKit, Unread, NetNewsWire, and many more. Your read state, saved stories, and training sync across every app.

            Do I need RSS feeds to follow a site?

            No. Web Feeds let you follow any website, even without RSS. NewsBlur monitors the page for changes and delivers new content to you automatically.

            How does NewsBlur compare to Feedly?

            NewsBlur offers intelligence training, full-text search, Ask AI, Web Feeds, native apps, and is fully open source. No ads, no tracking, no algorithmic feeds.

            See the full comparison →

            How does NewsBlur compare to Inoreader?

            NewsBlur gives you personal training instead of rules, built-in AI features, story clustering, and a fully open source codebase you can self-host.

            New Features

            May 15, 2026 Make NewsBlur match your home screen. Premium subscribers can choose a custom app icon on iOS, Mac, and Android, with 12 color flavors in light and dark variants. Read the blog post.
            Apr 23, 2026 Premium Pro regex classifiers now cover author bylines. On arXiv and other multi-author feeds, a pattern like Hinton|LeCun|Bengio catches any byline on a paper in one rule instead of fifteen.
            Apr 11, 2026 Story clustering now has two modes. Title mode groups only near-duplicate headlines, while Title + Related also pulls in loose topical matches. Each clustered sibling carries a Match or Related pill computed at read time against the story you’re viewing, so the label tracks actual title similarity rather than a feed-level tag.
